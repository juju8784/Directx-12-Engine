Events generated by GAudio.

These messages are sent when GAudio functions are called (including destructor).

EVENTS::PAUSE_SOUNDS                    Sent out when PauseSounds is called.

EVENTS::PAUSE_MUSIC                     Sent out when PauseMusic is called.
EVENTS::RESUME_MUSIC                    Sent out when ResumeMusic is called.

typedef forceRaw:: EVENT_DATA GW::AUDIO::GAudio::EVENT_DATA

EVENT_DATA holds the information about volume channels.

All events provide the following relevant information. 

EVENT_DATA::channelVolumes[6]           Array of per-channel volumes (size of 6 to support every case), used to transfer channel volume ratios/master volumes.

◆ SetSoundsChannelVolumes()

template<typename... Args>
GW::GReturn GW::AUDIO::GAudio::SetSoundsChannelVolumes ( Args &&...  parameter)

Sets Master Volume for all sounds for specific speakers (per channel).

The volume ratio in _values for _numChannels amount of channels. Sends the event to all existing sounds to update volumes in each channel with new master volume ratios per EACH channel passed up to _numChannels. If you attempt to set the volume of an output your hardware does not support, it will be ignored. Generally should be used with 2 (stereo) or 6 channels (5.1).

Channels: Supports up to 6 channels AKA 5.1 Audio: 

INDEX[0] = Left,
INDEX[1] = Right,
INDEX[2] = Front Center,
INDEX[3] = LFE,
INDEX[4] = Rear Left,
INDEX[5] = Rear Right,
Parameters
[in]_valuesThe array of volumes per channel (size MUST match the passed in _numChannels)
[in]_numChannelsThe number of channels affected.
Return values
INVALID_ARGUMENT_numChannels is less than 0 or greater than 6.
INVALID_ARGUMENT_values is NULL.
SUCCESSSuccessfully ran without running into any of the above issues.
